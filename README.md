🫀 Heart Disease Analysis Dashboard

Interactive Power BI dashboard that analyzes heart-failure patient records to measure survival outcomes and surface the clinical and demographic factors driving mortality risk. The report lets users slice every visual by gender and explore how survival shifts across age groups, turning a clinical dataset into a decision-ready tool for risk stratification.

📊 Key Visualizations

KPI Cards — Survival Snapshot. Headline metrics show a 67.89% survival rate, an average survival age of 58.76 years, and a 203-survived vs. 96-died split across 299 patients.
Survival Rate by Age Group. A breakdown showing survival falling from 75.90% in younger bands to 40.38% for patients 71+, isolating age as the dominant risk axis.
Serum Creatinine by Age Group. Survival count plotted against average serum creatinine, confirming the marker's clinical relevance to outcomes.
Ejection Fraction by Age Group. Survival count against average ejection fraction, a key predictor of heart-failure mortality.
Clinical Risk Indicators. Smoking, high blood pressure, anaemia, and diabetes distributed by age group to pinpoint where preventive intervention matters most.
Gender Slicer. A global filter that instantly recomputes the entire report for male vs. female cohorts.

🎯 Project Purpose

The dashboard helps clinicians and health analysts identify which patients face the highest mortality risk and where preventive care delivers the greatest return, replacing static spreadsheets with an interactive, filterable clinical view.

👥 Stakeholders


Clinicians & Cardiologists: Stratify patients by risk and target follow-up care.
Hospital Administrators: Allocate resources toward high-risk age bands.
Public Health Researchers: Study how comorbidities cluster with mortality.
Data Analysts: Reuse the DAX model and visual design as a healthcare-analytics template.


🏆 Achievements and Metrics


Surfaced age as the dominant survival driver (75.90% → 40.38% across age bands).
Validated ejection fraction and serum creatinine as outcome predictors directly in the report.
Located the 51–70 cluster where smoking, hypertension, anaemia, and diabetes concentrate.


🚀 How to Use

Open the .pbix file in Power BI Desktop, then use the gender slicer and age-group visuals to explore survival patterns. Hover for tooltips and cross-filter by clicking any chart element.

✅ Conclusion

A compact, interactive clinical dashboard that converts raw heart-failure records into actionable risk insight — useful for anyone working at the intersection of healthcare and data.
